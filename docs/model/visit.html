Visit — one performed service log

A visit is one service performed at a location on a date — the field record of work done, normalized from one ION service log. It is the "did" that the task ("should") predicts and the invoice ("billed") charges for. Billing consumes visits; it doesn't create them.

Two truths to hold at once ION's identity for a visit is its LogID. ION also records, on each log, the EventID (= the parent task) and the TaskInvoiceID (= the QBO invoice it billed under). Those two recorded ids are what let the per-day pull attribute and reconcile visits at 100% match — no guessing. Green pills = verified live; amber = drift; red = not built yet.

What it is & what it's telling us

Reading one visit answers: did we service this place on this day, who/when, was it billable, and what did we use? It carries the time on site, whether it counts as serviceable, the price it bills at, the task it belongs to, and — decomposed into child tables — the chemical readings, consumables used, and the task checklist.

Identity & grain

  • ION's identity = LogID (one row per service log) → stored as ion_log_id.
  • Our unique key = visits_uniq_log_natural on (service_location_id, scheduled_date, service_type, pool_id, started_at) NULLS NOT DISTINCT.
  • Several service bodies serviced under one service on one day collapse to one visit (billing collapses by task×day anyway); per-pool detail lives on the child rows.

Serviceable rule validated 2026-06-03

A visit was performed (and ION bills it) iff it has a time_in. The time_out may be missing (tech never clocked out) or garbled (AM/PM typo) yet still be a real billed visit. So:

serviceable = has time_in AND NOT (time_out present AND time_out == time_in)

Only an explicit zero-duration log (in == out) is a genuine skip/no-access. No time_in → not performed → the log path skips it entirely.

1 · Enumerate the day — list_day_logs(date_us)

Returnsevery log that day: log_id (LogID), calendar_id, customer_name, service_type, tech, completed (green bullet)
Endpoint/home/customerLogDetails.cfm?...&dayindexsel=MM/DD/YYYY&logset=1

2 · Authoritative detail — get_log_detail([{log_id, calendar_id}])

Returnsevent_id (EventID = task), task_invoice_id (TaskInvoiceID = QBO invoice DocNumber), time_in/time_out, serviceable, invoice_type, service_profile, consumables {item_id: qty}
Endpoint/tasks/addLog.cfm?calendarID=…&LogID=…

3 · Resolve & store — ingest_day_logs(start, end, dry_run=true)

  • Keep logs that were performed (time_in present) and resolve to a task (event_id). The green "completed" bullet is not the gate.
  • Resolve event_id → task_schedules.ion_task_id → task_id + service_location_id + rate. Deterministic lookup, not inference.
  • Price at the contracted ion.recurring_tasks.task_price_cents; fall back to the number parsed from the service name only when null.
  • Store via a scoped transactional replace over the window: delete the window's visits + consumables, re-insert one visit per performed log (external_source='ion_log'), then its consumables.
In code (Windmill — not mirrored to the repo) f/ION/ingest_day_logs → f/ION/api/list_day_logs + f/ION/api/get_log_detail. dry_run=true default.

Debugging guide — symptom → where to look

Orphan visit / missing from invoice A log with no event_id, or an event_id not in task_schedules, is dropped (log path) or stored unattributed (bulk path). Look: ingest_day_logs unresolved_events; maintenance.visits WHERE task_id IS NULL.
Visit has no readings or checklist Expected — ion_log-sourced visits don't carry chem_readings/visit_tasks yet (not extracted from addLog.cfm). Only ion (bulk) visits have them.
Price doesn't match the report The log path prices at the contracted rate (task_price_cents), not the log's shown number. A mismatch points at ion.recurring_tasks.task_price_cents for that task.
A visit "disappeared" after a re-run ingest_day_logs does a scoped replace (delete the window, re-insert). A log that stopped being "performed" (lost its time_in) won't come back. Look: the per-day built / not_performed counts in the run summary.
